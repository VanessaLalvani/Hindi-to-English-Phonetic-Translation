I am Indian, but I do not speak Hindi.

    I grew up inside the language by listening to my mother pray. I recognized rhythm and melody while meaning remained out of reach.

I learned to listen for sound and shape, for the texture of words I could recognize but not fully understand.

    This is the space between inheritance and access. Between knowing and almost-knowing.

Like striking a match in the dark,

phonetics

became my way in.

    Hindi scripts (Devanagari) breaks into three foundational parts:

Independent Vowels:

    sounds that stand alone

Dependent Vowels:

    sounds that attach to consonants

Consonants:

    the structural foundation of every word

This project recreates that process of learning through sound, not syntax. It mirrors the slow, uncertain work of reconstructing a language you were always around but never formally taught.

This website teaches you Devanagari letter paired with an English word that echoes its sound. It's a bridge between two scripts.

    Using audio cues, examples and labels, the website introduces a new style of phonetic learning

Each matchbox holds a letter. Each flame reveals a sound. Each strike is an act of reconnection.
