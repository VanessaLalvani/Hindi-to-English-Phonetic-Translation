Consonants

Consonants are the backbone of Hindi script, each one a complete syllable carrying an inherent "a" sound. They do not wait to be activated, they arrive ready, shaped by centuries of use. Rather than memorising abstract symbols, every consonant is paired with a real English word you already know. A transliteration bridge between two alphabets, two worlds.
